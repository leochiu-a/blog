My current goal is to spend as much time in my [higher self](/blog/clay-and-light) as possible.

But I struggle with how to achieve this.

In almost every avenue of life, we have systematic tools for achieving outcomes, what I often think of as ‘technology’. For example, **social technologies** include things like democracy, contracts and corporations that help define our social contract and encourage pro-social behavior.

Reading [Ghazali’s Disciplining the Soul](https://www.amazon.com/Disciplining-Soul-Breaking-Two-Desires/dp/0946621438) was the first time I truly internalized the idea of _spiritual technologies_. Systematized ways of improving ones character and soul.

He lists 4 in particular: solitude, silence, hunger and sleeplessness.


<BookQuote speaker="Imam Al-Ghazali" source="Disciplining the Soul">
Although the actual methods followed in self-discipline varied considerably, most of the early ascetics stressed four practices: ‘solitude, silence, hunger, and sleeplessness.‘
</BookQuote>


Through most of my life, I have thought of these as problems to be solved. Why be hungry, when you could eat? Why stay awake tired, when you could sleep?

The answer is _because_, it does not make sense. It is not for your lower self. It is in search of the euphoria of the higher self.

I think of these tools, the only ones I feel comfortable wielding are solitude and silence. I have rarely achieved the euphoria of fasting or of sleeplessness.

But I love to try new technology.
